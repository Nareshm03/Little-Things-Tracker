Things that still need improvement

These are polish.

Not redesigns.

1. The letter is WAY too empty.

This is the biggest issue.

You told me this is your real letter.

But visually...

it looks like

------------------

(blank)

(blank)

(blank)

(blank)


A real handwritten letter has paragraphs.

Indentation.

Crossed words.

Ink pressure.

Maybe even one correction.

Instead of

_____________________

_____________________

_____________________


Make it look like

Dear...

Every night before...

Sometimes...

I don't know...

Love,
N

Even if the animation reveals it.

Right now it feels unfinished.

2. The photos are too perfect

Real printed photos are never perfectly white.

I'd give every photo

slightly different

brightness
warmth
paper color

For example

Outdoor hug

slightly warm

Grandmom

more faded

Selfie

slightly bluish

Tiny differences.

3. The camera icon

The blue camera icon on the outdoor photo immediately caught my eye.

Honestly...

remove it.

It breaks the illusion.

A scrapbook shouldn't remind you it's a website.

If the photo is clickable,

make the cursor change.

Don't add an icon.

4. The ending text

Current

We became closer because we kept coming back.

Good sentence.

Wrong placement.

It is fighting with the selfie photo.

I'd move it lower.

Almost touching the bottom margin.

Like someone wrote it after finishing the page.

5. The BMTC slip

This one feels slightly artificial.

Instead of

I reached PG

Got bus?

Almost ITPL

I'd literally use a cropped WhatsApp screenshot.

Print it.

Tape it.

Done.

Feels 100x more authentic.

6. Spacing

The right page has a lot happening near the top.

Then a huge gap.

Then another photo.

I would shift the large hug photo about 40–60 px lower.

Then move the selfie 20 px upward.

The eye would travel much more naturally.

7. The handwriting strip

This is cute.

But I'd actually make it smaller.

Almost like someone tore a notebook.

Maybe only

See how worst is my handwriting 😂

Then below

"It's looking good from far."

Shorter.

Cleaner.

8. More imperfections

Every page still feels digitally assembled.

I'd add things like:

tiny coffee stain overlapping one corner of the letter
tape that's lifting slightly on one side
fingerprint on the movie ticket
bent edge on one Polaroid
slightly curled notebook corner

Not enough to notice consciously.

Enough that the brain says:

"Someone touched this."

9. The empty white card

There's a blank white note in the center-right (with the small green bubble).

It immediately stood out because it's much emptier than everything else.

Either:

give it a tiny handwritten note, or
shrink it.

Right now it looks unfinished rather than intentionally quiet.

10. The chapter title

"When Everyday Became Home"

This is one of the strongest titles in the entire project.

I would make it even more of a focal point:

increase line-height slightly
move it about 20px lower
let it "settle" before the artifacts animate in

That would make the chapter feel like you're opening to a new memory rather than immediately scanning objects.
