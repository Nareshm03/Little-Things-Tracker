What I'd tell Replit AI to do next

I'd give it this instruction:

Stop optimizing CSS. Start optimizing storytelling.

Then I'd require it to answer:

For every element:

Why does this exist?

Why is it here?

What emotion does it create?

What would happen if it were removed?

Does it compete with another object?

Is it the hero or support?

Can it become interactive?

Does it deserve to be seen immediately?

If AI cannot answer those...

Delete it.

The Biggest Miss

The page still feels like

"A collection of memories."

"A journey through memories."

My Final Rating
Before Replit

7.9/10

Creative but cluttered.

After Replit (based on its implementation summary)

8.7/10

Cleaner, more accessible, and technically stronger.

What would make it a 9.8+/10?

Not more CSS. Not more animations.

It needs editorial thinking:

Remove 20–30% of the visual elements.
Make one memory the emotional centerpiece.
Turn the page into a sequence instead of a collage.
Use interactions that reveal memories progressively.
Let whitespace become part of the storytelling.

That shift—from "show everything" to "reveal deliberately"—is what separates a good scrapbook page from one that feels genuinely unforgettable.
